Major Stock Exchange Indexes

Stock Indexs are great tools for navigating the stock market and getting a general idea of the marekt's performance. Below are three of the most comonly used indexs.

S&P 500

Created by Poor's Publishing and Standards Statistics in 1957. It is a makret-cap weighted index. It is composed of the 500 largest and most important U.S. Stocks.

NASDAQ Composite Index

Created by National Associate of Security Dealers (NASD). Market-Capitalization weighted index. It includes ~3,000 of all the stocks listed on NASDAQ Stock Exchang.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average

Created by Charles Dow and Edward Jones in 1896. Price-weighted index of 30 large, well established, and actively traded U.S. Stocks. Higher price stocks have a bigger impact than lwoer priced stocks.
